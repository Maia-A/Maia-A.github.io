Ancient Athens

The three philosophers lived, studied, and taught in Ancient Athens, which was the biggest and most powerful of the Greek city-states. It was named after Athena, the goddess of wisdom and war. Its people lived below a hill called the Acropolis on which they built a magnificent marble temple called the Parthenon.
