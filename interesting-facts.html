Interesting Facts

Unattractive, Poor, and Married to a Difficult Woman

According to Plato, Socrates was a short, stocky man with a snub nose and bulging eyes. He also went out barefoot, dirty, and disheveled. This upset Greeks, who valued beauty and proportion. Despite his less than ideal appearance, he was married to a woman named Xanthippe, who was more than two decades younger. Despite her youth, his wife was reported to be physically unattractive and shrewish. In one account, she emptied a chamber pot in Socrates’ head on a fit of anger!

According to Plato, Socrates refused payments for tutoring his students and therefore was very poor. Socrates’ Xanthippe was reportedly not happy when Socrates abandoned his earlier profession of stonecutting to become an unpaid philosopher. She complained that he did not make enough money to support his own family. Socrates did not consider himself a “teacher” because his method involved asking questions, not answering them. He did regard philosophers who were paid to instruct the rich and powerful people of Athens favorably and often made cutting remarks about them.

Not his real name?

Plato’s name might have been a nickname, since it means “broad and strong.” Some historians believe his real name was Aristocles. The controversies about him end there. He had some progressive ideas, the most romantic of which was that lovers are incomplete halves until they find each other. He reportedly said that there is “only one person in the world for me, without whom I am lost.” He believed that truth could be found through mathematics and geometry. Plato also believed in reincarnation: His theory was that after death, a soul rose to a higher plane through goodness, beauty, and justice.

Knew Everything but Knew nothing about equal rights

Aristotle contributed to every academic field imaginable, including astronomy, anatomy, geology, geography, meteorology, physics, politics, metaphysics, economics, rhetoric, psychology and theology, literature, poetry, and drama. As a result, he became known as “the man who knew everything.” He has also been called “the last person to know everything there was to know”because he knew everything there was to learn during his lifetime. Since then, knowledge has grown tremendously, so it’s impossible for anyone else to attain this title.

However, he believed women were inferior to men and that they did not have the ability to make logical decisions. He considered women as deceptive, complaining, and impulsive. Since Aristotle was respected at “the” philosopher for many centuries, his misogynistic views were used throughout history to deny the rights of women. To make matters worse, he believed the idea of certain people being “born to be slaves.” He argued that these people did not have “complete souls,” could not think clearly, and needed to be owned in order to live their lives.
