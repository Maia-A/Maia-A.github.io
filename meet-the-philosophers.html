Meet the Philosophers

Socrates, Plato, and Aristotle represent three generations of Greek philosophers. Born in 469 BCE, Socrates is considered to be the father of western philosophy. His student Plato, who was born in 428 BCE, was Socrates' biggest fan and wrote much about him. Plato in turn taught Aristotle, who was born in 385 BCE and went on to tutor Alexander the Great. The three philosophers impacted not only their own generation but also future generations, and their ideas continue to be studied today.

Remembering the correct order

It's easy: The initials of Socrates, Plato, and Aristotle in the correct order spell the word SPA.
