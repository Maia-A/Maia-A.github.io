What Is Philosophy?

The word “philosophy” is from the Greek word φιλοσοφία, phílosophía, which means “the love of wisdom.” Philosophers study knowledge and ask questions about the following topics: learning, reasoning, existence, proof, ethics, language, and many others. Ancient Greeks included the arts, religion, science and mathematics as part of the study of philosophy. Philosophy is a broad topic that has shaped many other fields of study, and it is difficult to define it in simple terms.

The following are some popular philosophical questions:
  • What is reality?
  • What is the purpose of life?
  • Can we really know anything?
  • What is happiness?
  • What is friendship?
  • Are our actions predestined?
  • Is death the end or a new beginning?
  • Is there such a thing as an ideal government?
  • Is belief the same as superstition?
  • Is there a perfect life?
